Sub categories of rats

Rat Name Uses
Rattus Hattus Rattus Hattus is usually farmed for his stylish hat, which is usually used as replacements for those mini umbrellas used at hotels. Their fur, when burned off their decaying corpse, can also be used as a seasoning for soups, salads, pancakes and rocks.
Rattus Phattus Rattus Phattus is often harvested for his body fat, that works as an efficent cooking oil. The average Rattus Phattus weighs about 10-11 pounds, yeilding about 3 cups of oil for one rat. Rattus Phattus's oil also can be drank to make your skin soft.
